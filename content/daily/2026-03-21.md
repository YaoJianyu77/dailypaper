### 1. Leveraging Large Language Models for Generalizing Peephole Optimizations

*arXiv preprint | 2026-03-19T04:19:51Z | Chunhao Liao, Hongxu Xu, Xintong Zhou, Zhenyang Xu et al.*

> An LLM-assisted framework generalizes compiler peephole optimizations through a closed loop that couples broader rule generation with formal validation and profitability checks.

Peephole optimization generalization is a compiler bottleneck because useful rewrites often start as narrow examples and must be lifted into rules that stay correct across wider instruction patterns. This paper proposes LPG, which uses an LLM to drive symbolic constant, structural, constraint, and bitwidth or precision generalization, then filters candidates with syntactic validation, semantic verification, and profitability checking. The abstract reports results on real LLVM peephole issues: LPG generalizes 90 of 102 optimizations overall, and 74 of 81 on an integer subset where Hydra generalizes 35. If those results hold under fair evaluation, the method could reduce manual effort in expanding compiler rewrite coverage. The biggest missing detail is how the verification pipeline, benchmark construction, and comparison protocol are implemented, so robustness and practical cost are still unclear from the abstract.

If the method is robust, it could lower the engineering cost of expanding compiler optimization coverage by combining LLM-driven abstraction with formal safety filters. A key caveat is that the paper is only indirectly relevant to LLM inference serving or CUDA kernel generation; the contribution is compiler-rule automation. Another caveat is that the abstract reports success counts but not compilation overhead, downstream code-quality impact, or false-positive behavior beyond stated checks.
