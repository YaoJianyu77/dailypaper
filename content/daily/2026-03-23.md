### 1. KPerfIR: Towards a Open and Compiler-centric Ecosystem for GPU Kernel Performance Tooling on Modern AI Workloads.

*OSDI | 2025 | Yue Guan 0003, Yuanwei Fang, Keren Zhou 0001, Corbin Robeck et al.*

> KPerfIR moves GPU kernel profiling into compiler passes, aiming to make Triton-based performance analysis more programmable, portable, and fine-grained for AI workloads.

GPU kernel optimization for modern AI workloads increasingly runs through compiler stacks such as Triton, but profiling is often still separated from the compiler pipeline. This paper proposes KPerfIR, a multilevel compiler-centric infrastructure that embeds profiling into the compiler workflow so profiling features can be implemented as reusable compiler passes. The abstract argues that this enables finer-grained analysis of hard GPU optimization issues, including overlap among fine-grained function units, and reports an implementation inside Triton. The evidence claimed in the abstract is concrete but limited to headline numbers: 8.2% overhead, 2% relative error, and actionable insight for complicated intra-kernel optimizations. The biggest missing detail is evaluation scope, because the abstract does not say which workloads, GPUs, baselines, or optimization cases were used.

If the approach generalizes, it could make GPU kernel performance analysis easier to customize and keep aligned with evolving AI compiler stacks and low-level optimization work. A key caveat is that the abstract does not show whether the results generalize beyond the evaluated kernels and GPUs. Another caveat is that the claimed optimization insight is qualitative in the abstract, so the practical debugging workflow is still unclear.
