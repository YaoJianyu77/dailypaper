### 1. DFlash: Block Diffusion for Flash Speculative Decoding

*arXiv preprint | 2026-02-05T18:59:30Z | Jian Chen, Yesheng Liang, Zhijian Liu*

> This paper replaces sequential autoregressive drafting in speculative decoding with a lightweight block diffusion model that proposes token blocks in one forward pass.

DFlash targets a core speculative-decoding bottleneck: in most prior methods, the draft stage is still sequential, so end-to-end gains remain limited even when verification is parallel. It uses a lightweight block diffusion draft model, conditioned on context features from the target model, to generate draft tokens in a single pass and improve acceptance while keeping decoding lossless. The abstract reports over 6x acceleration across models and tasks and up to 2.5x more speedup than EAGLE-3, but it does not make the hardware setup, draft-model overhead, or baseline fairness clear.

### 2. Multi-Token Prediction via Self-Distillation

*arXiv preprint | 2026-02-05T18:54:48Z | John Kirchenbauer, Abhimanyu Hans, Brian Bartoldson, Micah Goldblum et al.*

> Turns a pretrained autoregressive LM into a standalone multi-token predictor with online self-distillation, aiming to capture decode speedups without auxiliary models or a specialized serving pipeline.

The paper proposes converting a standard next-token LM into a standalone multi-token predictor through an online self-distillation objective, while keeping deployment identical to the original checkpoint and avoiding speculator-verifier pipelines. The abstract presents this as a simpler alternative to speculative decoding and reports more than 3x average decoding speedup on GSM8K with under 5% accuracy loss relative to single-token decoding. The main unresolved points are how the multi-token objective is implemented, what runtime conditions produce the gain, and how fairly it compares against other acceleration baselines.

### 3. Protean Compiler: An Agile Framework to Drive Fine-grain Phase Ordering

*arXiv preprint | 2026-02-05T19:24:05Z | Amir H. Ashouri, Shayan Shirahmad Gale Bagi, Kavin Satheeskumar, Tejas Srikanth et al.*

> An LLVM-integrated framework for fine-grained phase ordering that exposes static features and pluggable ML or LLM hooks, with reported gains over O3 on Cbench.

This paper presents Protean Compiler, a version of LLVM with built-in fine-grained phase-ordering support rather than an external tuning loop. The main mechanism is a large static-feature library plus interfaces for third-party ML frameworks and LLM-driven two-step optimization. The abstract reports modest average gains over O3 on Cbench, larger wins on selected applications, and a small build-time cost, but it leaves evaluation breadth, baseline fairness, and transfer beyond Cbench unclear.

### 4. Stop the Flip-Flop: Context-Preserving Verification for Fast Revocable Diffusion Decoding

*arXiv preprint | 2026-02-05T19:58:48Z | Yanzheng Xiang, Lan Wei, Yizhen Yao, Qinglin Zhu et al.*

> The paper targets a specific inefficiency in revocable diffusion decoding by preserving context during verification so repeated remask-restore cycles do not erase useful parallel progress.

The paper studies a failure mode in parallel diffusion decoding where revocable verification causes flip-flop oscillations: tokens are remasked and later restored unchanged, which both weakens conditioning context and wastes revision budget. It proposes COVER, a single-pass verification scheme that uses KV cache override to verify selected seeds while preserving their context for other queries, plus stability-aware seed selection and an adaptive verification count. The abstract claims faster decoding with preserved output quality across benchmarks, but it does not specify the benchmark setup, baseline fairness, or the size of the gains.
